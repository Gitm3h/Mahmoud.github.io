Education

During my 120-hour TEFL certification course at ITTT, I gained essential skills in classroom management, lesson planning, and the Engage, Study, Activate (ESA) methodology. I learned to structure lessons effectively, tailoring them to diverse learning styles and clear objectives. The ESA model equipped me to captivate students during the Engage phase, teach grammar and vocabulary in the Study phase, and foster real-world language use in the Activate phase. Additionally, the course emphasized providing constructive feedback and fostering cultural sensitivity, ensuring my teaching is inclusive and impactful.

I hold a Bachelor's degree in English Literature and Languages from the Lebanese University, where I developed a deep understanding of literary analysis, critical thinking, and effective communication. My studies covered a wide range of genres, periods, and cultural contexts, enriching my ability to interpret texts and appreciate diverse perspectives. Additionally, the program sharpened my writing skills, from academic essays to creative expression, and provided me with a strong foundation in linguistics, language acquisition, and pedagogy.

I hold a B2 certificate in German, which demonstrates my ability to communicate effectively in diverse professional and social contexts. As a native Arabic speaker with near-native proficiency in English, my multilingual abilities enable me to bridge cultural and linguistic gaps, fostering meaningful connections in global settings. The process of mastering multiple languages has enhanced my communication skills, cultural awareness, and adaptability—qualities that are invaluable in professional environments.
